Week 6 · Vision • Three tracks
The main event
Defining
milestones.
Testable checkpoints, not feature wishlists
Before we start

What makes a good milestone.

Demonstrable

You can show it to someone. "Look, when I hover over a household, this happens."

Testable

You know when it is done. Clear acceptance criteria, not vibes.

Incremental

Useful even if the track isn't finished. Each milestone stands on its own.

Sprint-sized

1-2 sprints of work for the team. Not a quarter-long epic.

Bad milestone: "Make the UI better."

Good milestone: "Household hover shows MFAI score, income, and store count on click."

Week 6 · Sprints • Breakdown
Commitment

What are you shipping this week?

Each person commits to at least one issue. Write your name next to it.

Milestone lead

Not who does all the work. Who is responsible for it getting done, coordinating across issues, and flagging when it is stuck.

Issue owner

You claimed it, you ship it. Full pipeline: spec, plan, build, test, review, merge. Same process as always.

Beyond this project: Roadmaps that do not have names next to items do not get done. Shared responsibility is no responsibility. The lead is not doing more work. They are making sure the work happens.
Week 6 · Sprints • Commitment
After today

The structure stays. The sessions shift.

What stays the same

  • Sprint structure with labels
  • Four-step review process on every PR
  • Project board as source of truth
  • Spec, plan, execute, verify workflow
  • The "explain every line" rule

What changes

  • Sessions shift from teaching to coordination
  • Quick standups, unblocking, sprint planning
  • You drive the roadmap, not the slides
  • Milestone leads own their track's progress
  • PR cleanup is a continuous expectation
